steno

how do you speak: in letters or in words&phrases?

now, isn’t that strange that we have to press each letter continiously, to say something?

also, we have an ability to press multiple buttons at once, yet 99.9% of the time we press only one key.

is where a faster, leaner, cleaner way to do it?

as you guessed (if you didn’t, then poor you), I mean stenotyping, which uses a “sound keyboard” (steno guys don’t call it like that, but who cares anyway) to write words and phrases.
that’s a steno machine by Stenograph™

stenotype machines are mostly used in the courts — when the jury, the judge, the defendant & the plaintiff talk simultaneously the talk turns into such a mishmash that robots can’t understand a single sentence. yet a human with a stenotype machine can (and will) write all of it in a matter of seconds.

unfortunately, most of us cannot afford using one of them — firstly, the price is extremely high ($4000), secondly — you need to relearn the whole typing experience again.

if you really want to try how this works, you may use plover (rhymes with “lover”). sadly, I can’t use it — I need to use three languages on one board (and russian is not supported much).

pedals

yep. the next big thing that improves writing is pedals.
like in a comfy car.

no more do you have to press&hold the modificators with your hands: leave that work to your legs!

jokes aside, using pedals is awesome — the only problem I’ve met with mine ($60 for these) is the setup. windows was required. crap.

so I recommend you to look at the car simulator pedals (used ones), as they are much cheaper even with an analog→digital converter.

my preferred layout is minimak (4 key — etdk→dkte), because it is similar to qwerty, but much more savvy to your fingers.
